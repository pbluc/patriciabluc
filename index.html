Bio
I am an undergraduate Computer Science student in the Fu Foundation School of Engineering and Applied Science at Columbia University in the City of New York. I am currently a Computer Science Instructor at Juni Learning, an online academy that offers private, online courses to students, and volunteering as a Curriculum Developer for CodeConnects, the online learning initiative of The Coding School, a non-profit that aims to empower the next generation through computer science education. I am interested in artificial intelligence systems, particularly wanting to build systems that are able to learn and grow in the same capacity as humans.
Research

Teacher and Student Responses to Online Teaching During Covid-19

This presentation looks at survey results and analysis concerning one high school’s reflection of the online experience due to schools closing with the onset of Covid-19. A teacher (PhD in multicultural education and literature) from the school reports on the teacher findings, and one of the teacher’s students (graduate of 2020) reports on the student findings. Keywords: Online Teaching/Learning, Pandemic, Teacher and Administrative Support





Constructing and Implementing A Bioengineered Technology Model That Replaces Sleep

Conducting a year-long research-based investigation consisting of a qualitative content analysis and survey method through which the researcher explores the biological phenomena that is sleep and ultimately designs a model of a potential biotechnology that can be used to prolong wakefulness. Research mentor throughout was Associate Professor (Research) Dr. Jamie Marc Zeitzer for Stanford Center for Sleep Sciences and Medicine. Keywords: Sleep, Wakefulness, Biotechnology
