Bio
I am an undergraduate student studying Computer on the Intelligent Systems Track in the Fu Foundation School of Engineering and Applied Science at Columbia University in the City of New York.

Currently, I am working at the Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory under the M²LInES (Multiscale Machine Learning In Coupled Earth System Modeling) research group as an affiliate student lab assistant working to improve climate projections, using machine learning–interfaced through Python–to capture physical processes at the oceanographic level. I am interested in designing artificial intelligence systems, particularly wanting to build systems that are able to learn and grow in the same capacity as humans.

I also have quite some passion for mixed reality (MR) technology, which includes virtual and augmented reality (VR/AR). From building my own fleshed-out MR-based mobile apps to working within Meta’s Reality Lab, I’ve built up a diverse portfolio range within this area of interest. I hope to bring the VR and AR experiences and innovations of science fiction to the forefront of the technologically driven future currently being paved.
Research

Teacher and Student Responses to Online Teaching During Covid-19

This presentation looks at survey results and analysis concerning one high school’s reflection of the online experience due to schools closing with the onset of Covid-19. A teacher (PhD in multicultural education and literature) from the school reports on the teacher findings, and one of the teacher’s students (graduate of 2020) reports on the student findings.

Keywords: Online Teaching/Learning, Pandemic, Teacher and Administrative Support





Constructing and Implementing A Bioengineered Technology Model That Replaces Sleep

Conducting a year-long research-based investigation consisting of a qualitative content analysis and survey method through which the researcher explores the biological phenomena that is sleep and ultimately designs a model of a potential biotechnology that can be used to prolong wakefulness. Research mentor: Associate Professor (Research) Dr. Jamie Marc Zeitzer for Stanford Center for Sleep Sciences and Medicine.

Keywords: Sleep, Wakefulness, Biotechnology
